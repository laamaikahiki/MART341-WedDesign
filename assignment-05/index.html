The Three Little Pigs

James Halliwell-Phillipps

Once upon a time, there were three little pigs and the time came for them to seek their fortunes to build their houses. The first little pig built him a house made of straw. The middle brother decided to build his house of sticks. Due to the first two houses not being strong, the third pig - the oldest brother decided to build his house of brick. He wanted a strong house because he heard of the wolf nearby that liked to catch little pigs and eat them. When the three little houses were completed, the all celebrated and cheered with joy while screaming, "Who's scared of furious wolf, furious wolf, furious wolf? Who's scared of furious wolf?" After their joyful screams, they all headed back to their newly built houses.

Just as the first pig got to his house, he was startled by the big bad wolf. He squealed and screamed as he quickly slammed his door. Wit a furious voice, the big bad wolf cried, "Little pig, little pig, let me come in." "Not by the hair of my chinny-chin-chin," said the little pig. "Then I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow your house in," said the wolf. The wolf blew as hard as he could and down came the house. The little pig ran as quick as he cold to his brothers house made of sticks.

The wolf was furious as he approached the middle brothers house that was made of stick. The little pig and his brother were singing, "Who's scared of furious wolf, furious wolf, furious wolf? Who's scared of furious wolf?" The furious wolf roared as he opened the door and said, "Let me come in." "No we won't," said the pigs. "Then I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow your house in," said the wolf. A strong blow from the wolf and there went the middle brothers house. Both pigs ran as fast as they could to their older brothers house that was made of bricks.
